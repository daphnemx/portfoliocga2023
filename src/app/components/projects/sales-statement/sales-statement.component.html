Overview

The project focused on renewing the Sales Statement app within IBM's main Sales Team payment and reporting system. This app provided monthly sales sales statements to the global sales team. The main challenge I faced as Product Strategist was to design an adaptable solution that catered to different geographies while effectively addressing users' pain points and deliver a renewed, user-friendly interface.

Research and findings

I implemented a comprehensive research approach to enhance the Sales Statement app. The research strategies I decided to apply were:

  • Heuristic evaluation of former interface: to discover and validate reported pain points.
  • User interviews: interview end users is my favorite research method, it allows me to empathize with them and know from first hand their needs and expectations for the final product.
  • Feedback survey analysis: I personally conducted a qualitative analysis of the comments collected in the former interface to gain a deeper understanding of specific user frustrations and preferences.

The research revealed challenges related to the clarity and transparency of payment breakdown information, directly affecting sellers' commission payments.

Sellers found it challenging to understand the payment breakdown information due to its lack of clarity. This resulted in confusion and frustration, as commissions were a vital aspect of their compensation. The interviews and feedback analysis make clear to me the importance of improving the overall user experience and addressing the specific pain points related to payment statement clarity.

Requirements gathering process

I engaged the stakeholders through collaborative meetings to enhance the research findings. These interactions provided valuable insights into business goals, legal requirements, and the diverse needs of various regions.It was crucial to design a solution that not only satisfied user needs but also aligned with specific legal regulations in each country or region.

Analyzing these research findings made evident to me that the renewed app had to tackle the issue of unclear payment breakdown information, adapt to different geographical contexts, and comply with legal requirements. These insights formed the basis for the subsequent design and testing phases of the project.

Design and testing

I utilized IBM's Carbon Design System to create the screens in Figma that maintained consistency with the overall look and feel of the product. The design aimed to tackle the challenge of providing clear and informative payment breakdown information, a significant pain point for sellers due to its impact on commission payments. I conducted user testing and employed iterative design processes to refine the user experience while considering user needs and business goals.

By conducting usability tests and engaging in design playbacks with stakeholders, I actively shaped the design iterations to ensure alignment with user needs and business goals. The insights gained from these collaborative efforts were invaluable in refining the design, ultimately delivering a user-centered solution that met both functional and strategic requirements. By actively incorporating feedback from testing and maintaining ongoing engagement with stakeholders, the design process resulted in a more effective and purposeful outcome.

Results and key learnings

The renewed Sales Statement app successfully addressed the pain points identified during the research phase. By improving the clarity of payment breakdown information, sellers could confidently understand their commissions, reducing frustration and improving overall satisfaction.

The project highlighted to me the significance of conducting thorough research, involving stakeholders, and iteratively testing designs to achieve a user-centered solution. The learnings from this project reinforced on me the importance of understanding user needs, aligning with business requirements, and continuously iterating to deliver a high-quality product.
